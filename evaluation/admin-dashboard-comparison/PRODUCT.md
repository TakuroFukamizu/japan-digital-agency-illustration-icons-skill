# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary users are municipal operations staff who review and process many online applications during a normal office day. The comparison is also used by skill authors and frontend developers evaluating whether the Digital Agency asset skill materially improves implementation quality.

## Product Purpose

Provide a controlled side-by-side comparison of the same dense application-management dashboard built without and with the `digital-agency-illustration-icons` skill. Success means reviewers can identify concrete differences in asset choice, consistency, accessibility, and implementation without layout or data changes confounding the result.

## Positioning

A reproducible A/B fixture that makes the skill's influence visible in a realistic Japanese public-service admin interface.

## Brand Personality

Operational, trustworthy, and composed. The dashboard should feel familiar to experienced back-office staff and remain readable during long sessions.

## Anti-references

Avoid sparse landing-page layouts, decorative government motifs, oversized metrics, playful consumer-app styling, and intentionally degraded baseline code. The baseline must remain a plausible competent implementation.

## Design Principles

- Hold information architecture, copy, data, and viewport constant across both variants.
- Make differences traceable to asset selection and skill guidance.
- Favor scan speed and state clarity over visual novelty.
- Keep all critical meaning in text and use imagery as reinforcement.
- Preserve a realistic level of administrative density without creating visual chaos.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard focus, visible labels, non-color status cues, reduced motion, responsive navigation, and horizontally scrollable tables without hiding essential controls.
